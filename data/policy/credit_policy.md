# Commercial Credit Policy — Small & Mid-Market Business Lending

Document version: 4.2 | Effective: 2026-01-01 | Owner: Credit Risk Committee

This policy governs the underwriting of commercial term loans and lines of credit
up to USD 10,000,000. Each numbered clause is a discrete underwriting rule. Where a
loan fails a hard threshold, the application must be declined or referred per the
clause. Where a loan fails a soft threshold, an exception may be granted under
Section 7.

---

## 1. Eligibility

- **CP-1.1** The applicant must be a legally registered business operating for at
  least **two (2) full fiscal years** with financial statements covering that period.
- **CP-1.2** The requested facility must not exceed **USD 10,000,000**.
- **CP-1.3** Startups (under two years of operating history) are **declined** under
  this policy and must be referred to the Specialty Lending desk.

## 2. Liquidity Requirements

- **CP-2.1** Minimum **current ratio of 1.20**. Below 1.20 is a soft fail
  (exception eligible). Below **1.00 is a hard fail** and must be declined or
  referred to a senior analyst.
- **CP-2.2** Minimum **quick ratio of 0.70** for non-retail borrowers; retail and
  inventory-heavy borrowers may operate at **0.50** given inventory turnover.

## 3. Leverage Requirements

- **CP-3.1** Maximum **debt-to-equity of 3.0x**. Above 3.0x is a hard fail
  (decline or refer). Between 2.0x and 3.0x requires an exception and additional
  collateral.
- **CP-3.2** Maximum **debt-to-assets of 0.70**. Above 0.70 requires senior
  analyst review.
- **CP-3.3** Minimum **interest coverage (EBIT / interest) of 2.0x**. Below 2.0x is
  a hard fail. Between 2.0x and 3.0x is acceptable only with conditions
  (e.g., a debt service reserve).

## 4. Profitability Requirements

- **CP-4.1** The borrower must be **net profitable in the most recent fiscal year**.
  A net loss in the most recent year is a soft fail requiring an exception and a
  documented turnaround plan.
- **CP-4.2** Two consecutive years of net losses is a **hard fail** (decline).
- **CP-4.3** Gross margin materially below the industry benchmark (more than
  **1.5 standard deviations** below sector mean) must be explained in the memo.

## 5. Collateral & Structure

- **CP-5.1** Equipment and real-estate financing must be secured by a **first lien**
  on the financed asset.
- **CP-5.2** Unsecured facilities above **USD 1,000,000** require either a personal
  guarantee from owners holding 20%+ equity, or a compensating deposit relationship.
- **CP-5.3** Loan term must not exceed the **useful economic life** of the financed
  asset. Equipment loans are capped at **84 months**.

## 6. Relationship & Character

- **CP-6.1** New-to-bank borrowers requesting unsecured facilities above
  USD 1,000,000 are escalated to **Approve with conditions** at best, pending a
  personal guarantee.
- **CP-6.2** Any prior delinquency over 30 days within 24 months is a referral
  trigger.

## 7. Exceptions

- **CP-7.1** Exceptions to soft fails may be approved when **mitigating factors**
  exist: strong collateral coverage (LTV below 70%), a guarantor, a debt service
  reserve, or a committed contract backlog.
- **CP-7.2** Every exception must be **explicitly documented** in the credit memo
  with the compensating factor and the approving authority.
- **CP-7.3** **Hard fails cannot be waived by exception** and must be declined or
  referred to the Credit Risk Committee.

## 8. Risk Rating & Recommendation Guidelines

- **CP-8.1** **Low risk** — meets all hard and soft thresholds; ratios at or above
  sector benchmarks. Recommend **Approve**.
- **CP-8.2** **Medium risk** — one or more soft fails offset by mitigating factors,
  or ratios within ~1 standard deviation of benchmark. Recommend **Approve with
  conditions**.
- **CP-8.3** **High risk** — any hard fail, two or more unmitigated soft fails, or
  net loss in the most recent year. Recommend **Decline** or **Refer to senior
  analyst** depending on the severity and mitigants.
- **CP-8.4** Any single hard fail caps the recommendation at **Refer to senior
  analyst**; it may not be auto-approved.
